    For final, you will be required to present three code-based animation sketches in a single website. The requirements are the following:

  1. At least one sketch should be partially influenced by mouse on a real time basis. Some examples are, live drawing, mouseClick giving birth to object, mouseMove triggering objects to jitter or shift or change color/opacity.
  2. At least one sketch should be object-oriented, meaning there is an object class, based on which object is being declared and initiated.
  3. At least one sketch should have an object array, with objects stored inside. And a draw loop calling each object to perform various tasks.
  4. I strongly encourage you to keep building off of the existing midterm project. You will very likely to have better results and a more fully developed theme.
  5. Working in group of two is allowed, and very much encouraged. If you do so, tell me at least a week beforehand. Both of you will receive the same grade for the final project regardless of division of labor.
  6. On May 18th, you will be continuing the same presentation format used during midterm. Be respectiful of your fellow presenters. Absolutely no electronic devices allowed during the entire presentation.

    Presentation guideline revisited:

  1. What story are you telling?
  2. How you want your audience to feel?
  3. What's the inspiration? Show image or short video if you have them.
  4. Now, take the audience through each sketch you made. Code logic, structure, usage of color, image, video if any. And be prepared to explain how the code functions.
