Course Description, goals and objectives

User input can affect a wide range of new media artworks that may include animation, sound, web projects, and robotics. In this hands-on introduction, students will learn techniques for harnessing analog and digital inputs to create responsive, interactive artworks. Students will explore functional, ethical, and aesthetic frameworks for interactivity; develop skills to conceptualize, code, and craft their own interactive projects; and discover how to apply digital interactivity in engaging, participatory artworks. Through projects, experiments, lectures, readings, and field trips, this course introduces New Media Arts students to programming and microcontrollers; no previous programming experience is necessary. Prerequisite: NMA 2050, Introduction to New Media Arts.

Textbooks

The main textbook for this class is Learning Processing. Students with previous coding background are encouraged to read his more advanced series, Nature of Codeby Daniel Shiffman.

Excerpts from the book Multimedia - From Wagner to Virtual Reality will be assigned as reading assignments, and serve as reference for in-class discussions on the following week.

Homework

A coding assigment will be assigned after each lecture and collected at the begining of the following lecture. All students are requried to present their assigments to the rest of the class, articulating the conceptual, asthetic as well as technical aspect of the assignment.

Midterm and Final Projects

There will be a screen-based midterm project and a live-performance based final project assignment for this class. Students are highly encouraged to work in groups of 2 or 3. In-class discussions sessions as well as individual group meetings will be held to help conceptualize the midterm project idea. A written final project proposal will be assigned to help students draft ideas, gather visual and technical reference.
